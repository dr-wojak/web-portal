Governmentality 2025

Administrative Integrity, Accountability, and the Problem of Non-Response

Brøek Meinhardt Operations Research (O.R.) Independent

Executive Summary

This brief documents a sustained pattern of administrative non-response across formal governance channels over an extended period.

The issue is not one of policy disagreement but of procedural failure: the absence of determination, acknowledgement, or structured evaluation in response to formally submitted material.

1. The Core Problem

Where institutional responsibility exists but response does not, a structural condition emerges: accountability becomes formally defined but practically unavailable.

This condition raises a fundamental governance question: whether procedural obligations retain meaning if they are not enforced.

2. Analytical Frame

The present record is best understood not as a sequence of isolated failures but as a systemic pattern.

Definition: Administrative non-response is treated here as a functional outcome of institutional design rather than individual error.

3. Legal Context

  • Right to good administration
  • Duty to provide reasons
  • Right to an effective remedy
  • Principle of institutional accountability

4. Implications

Sustained non-response does not remain neutral. It produces downstream effects in law, governance credibility, and institutional legitimacy.

Notes

  1. Administrative obligation implies response, not mere receipt.
  2. Procedural silence may function as a de facto decision.
