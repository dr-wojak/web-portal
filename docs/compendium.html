Preamble

This compendium maps every party with a cognisable interest in the protracted failure to engage with Operations Research (O.R.) in Irish public governance — a failure documented continuously from November 11th, 2006 to the present.

As Ireland holds the Presidency of the Council of the European Union in the second half of 2026, stakeholder exposure extends beyond domestic administrative law into EU-level governance architecture.

Judicial Review — Prefatory Legal Question

Two distinct EU roles must be distinguished:

  • Presidency of the Council of the European Union (rotating)
  • President of the European Council (permanent)
Can a Member State lawfully hold the Council Presidency while suppressing an ethics complaint documenting its own breach of Treaty obligations?
